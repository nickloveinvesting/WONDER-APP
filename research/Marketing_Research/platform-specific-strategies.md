## Product Launch Platforms

### Product Hunt Strategy

**What Works**:
- Clear, compelling tagline
- Beautiful screenshots and GIFs
- Active maker engagement in comments
- Exclusive launch-day offers
- Technical innovation highlights
- Tuesday-Thursday launches
- Prepared hunter/supporter network

**Launch Approach for ARGUED**:
1. **Pre-Launch (2 weeks before)**:
   - Build Product Hunt profile
   - Engage with other products daily
   - Prepare assets: 4 screenshots, 2 GIFs, thumbnail
   - Write compelling description
   - Line up 10-15 supporters for early upvotes
   - Create exclusive PH offer: "Product Hunt Badge" or early access features

2. **Launch Day**:
   - Post at 12:01 AM PST for full 24-hour window
   - Immediate maker comment explaining the "why":
     - "Philosophy forums are gatekeep-y and hostile to beginners"
     - "Reddit discussions disappear in 24 hours"
     - "We built ARGUED to make philosophical discourse accessible and persistent"
   - Respond to EVERY comment within 1 hour
   - Share on Twitter, LinkedIn, relevant Discords

3. **Content Elements**:
   - **Tagline**: "Where philosophical conversations flourish"
   - **Description**: Focus on problems solved (gatekeeping, ephemeral discussions, hostile debates)
   - **Screenshots**: Show UI beauty, AI facilitation, discussion threads, Socratic dialogue
   - **First Comment**: Personal story of why you built it
   - **Offer**: "Product Hunt supporters get founding member badge + early access to AI features"

**What to Avoid**:
- Launching Monday or Friday
- Vote manipulation (ban risk)
- Ignoring comments
- Pure marketing speak
- Technical jargon without explanation

**Success Metrics**: Top 5 product of the day = ~500-1000 signups

## Tech Community Platforms

### Hacker News Strategy

**What Works**:
- Authentic, humble tone
- Technical innovation focus
- Engaging with every comment
- Sunday evening Show HN posts
- Showing, not selling
- Nerdy technical details

**Approach for ARGUED**:
1. **Show HN Post**:
   - **Title**: "Show HN: ARGUED – Platform for philosophical discussions with AI facilitation"
   - **Content**:
     ```
     Hey HN! I built ARGUED (argued.app) to solve problems I experienced in online philosophy discussions.

     Problems:
     - Philosophy forums are hostile to beginners
     - Reddit discussions vanish after 24 hours
     - Most platforms optimize for quick takes, not deep thought
     - Good arguments get lost in comment threads

     Features:
     - Persistent discussion threads with structure
     - AI-facilitated Socratic dialogue
     - Argument mapping and visualization
     - Reading groups and structured debates
     - Beginner-friendly onboarding

     Tech: [Your stack]

     I'd love feedback from the HN community. What would make you actually use a philosophy discussion platform?
     ```

2. **Timing**: Sunday 7-9 PM PST

3. **Engagement**:
   - Respond to every comment
   - Be humble and receptive to criticism
   - Share technical details when asked
   - Don't be defensive about negative feedback

**What to Avoid**:
- Marketing speak ("revolutionary," "game-changing")
- Ignoring technical questions
- Being defensive
- Asking for upvotes
- Reposting if it doesn't do well (ban risk)

**Success Metrics**: Front page = 5,000-20,000 visits, 500-2,000 signups

**Expect**: Harsh criticism, technical questions, some dismissiveness

## Key Success Patterns Across Platforms

### Universal Winning Strategies:
1. **Authenticity**: Personal story beats corporate speak
2. **Value First**: Help before promoting
3. **Community Participation**: Be a member, not just a promoter
4. **Engagement**: Respond to every comment/question
5. **Problem Focus**: Emphasize problems solved, not features
6. **Exclusive Offers**: Platform-specific benefits
7. **Patience**: Build presence before promoting

### Universal Mistakes to Avoid:
1. **Spam**: Copy-paste promotional posts
2. **Ignoring Rules**: Not reading community guidelines
3. **Ghost Posting**: Post and disappear
4. **Marketing Speak**: Buzzwords and hype
5. **Defensiveness**: Arguing with criticism
6. **Vote Manipulation**: Artificial engagement
7. **Fake Metrics**: Inflated or misleading numbers

## Measurement & Iteration

**Track for Each Platform**:
- Visits from platform
- Signup conversion rate
- User quality (engagement metrics)
- Time spent on site
- Return visit rate
- Cost (time investment)

**Adjust Strategy Based On**:
- Which platforms drive highest quality users
- Where community is most receptive
- Which content resonates most
- Where you can build sustainable presence

**Double Down On**:
- Platforms with >5% signup conversion
- Communities with high engagement
- Channels with recurring traffic
