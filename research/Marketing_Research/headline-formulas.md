## Problem-Solution Headlines

### Formula 1: "Tired of X? Here's Y"

**Structure**: `Tired of [pain point]? Here's [solution]`

**Psychology**: Validates frustration, promises relief

**Platform fit**: Reddit, Twitter, Medium

**Emotional trigger**: Frustration → Hope

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "Tired of philosophy discussions dying after 3 replies? Here's a better way"
- "Tired of philosophy feeling gatekept? Here's a platform for everyone"
- "Tired of Discord philosophy debates getting lost? Here's the fix"

**Variation**:
- "Sick of [X]? Try [Y]"
- "Done with [X]? [Y] solves it"

---

### Formula 2: "X is broken. Here's my attempt to fix it"

**Structure**: `[System/Platform] is broken. Here's my attempt to fix it`

**Psychology**: Identifies with shared frustration, shows humility

**Platform fit**: Hacker News, Indie Hackers, Reddit

**Emotional trigger**: Frustration → Curiosity about solution

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "Philosophy forums are broken. Here's my attempt to fix them"
- "Online philosophical discourse is dying. Here's my attempt to save it"
- "Philosophy discussions are inaccessible. Here's my attempt to change that"

**Why it works**:
- "My attempt" shows humility (not claiming success)
- "Broken" validates community frustration
- Invites others to judge if you succeeded

---

### Formula 3: "I struggled with X for Y time. Here's what worked"

**Structure**: `I struggled with [problem] for [time period]. Here's what worked`

**Psychology**: Personal experience = credibility, hope for others

**Platform fit**: Medium, Reddit, Indie Hackers

**Emotional trigger**: Empathy → Hope

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "I struggled to find quality philosophy discussions for years. Here's what worked"
- "I spent 3 years in academic philosophy and still couldn't discuss ideas freely. Here's what I built"
- "I struggled to make philosophy accessible for 5 years. Here's the result"

**Variation**:
- "After X years of Y, I finally solved Z"
- "Here's what X years of Y taught me about Z"

---

### Formula 4: "[Problem]. [Solution]. [Proof/Outcome]"

**Structure**: `[Specific problem]. [What you built]. [Early result/validation]`

**Psychology**: Clear, concise, proven

**Platform fit**: Twitter/X, LinkedIn

**Emotional trigger**: Efficiency (respects reader's time)

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "Philosophy discussions die in forum threads. Built ARGUED to keep them alive. 200+ debates going strong"
- "Online philosophy is gatekept. Made it accessible. Students and academics both using it"
- "Discord philosophy chats get lost. Created persistent discussions. 0% conversation abandonment"

**Why it works**:
- No fluff, all signal
- Third sentence provides social proof
- Easy to scan

---

### Formula 5: "The [adjective] way to [achieve goal]"

**Structure**: `The [better/easier/modern] way to [desired outcome]`

**Psychology**: Implies improvement over status quo

**Platform fit**: Product Hunt, Twitter, Reddit

**Emotional trigger**: Curiosity about improvement

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "The modern way to discuss philosophy online"
- "The accessible way to debate philosophy"
- "The structured way to have philosophical conversations"

**Avoid**: Superlatives (best, fastest) on Hacker News

**Better for HN**:
- "A modern alternative to philosophy forums"
- "An open-source platform for philosophy discussions"

**Emotional trigger**: Frustration → Relief

## Question-Based Headlines

### Formula 12: "Where do you X?"

**Structure**: `Where do you [do desired activity]?`

**Psychology**: Invites community input, validates problem through consensus

**Platform fit**: Reddit (excellent), Twitter, forums

**Emotional trigger**: Community belonging, validation

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "Where do you actually discuss philosophy online?"
- "Where do you debate philosophy without it getting gatekept?"
- "Where do you have in-depth philosophical conversations?"

**Follow-up pattern**: Share pain points, then mention building solution

**Why it works**:
- Not promotional (genuinely asking)
- Others share frustrations
- Validates your solution

---

## Technical/Show HN Headlines

### Formula 17: "Show HN: [Name] – [Technical description]"

**Structure**: `Show HN: [Product Name] – [Specific technical/functional description]`

**Psychology**: Directness, clarity, technical credibility

**Platform fit**: Hacker News (required format)

**Emotional trigger**: None (HN values information over emotion)

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "Show HN: ARGUED – Open-source philosophy discussion platform"
- "Show HN: ARGUED – Persistent philosophical debates with AI facilitation"
- "Show HN: ARGUED – Modern alternative to philosophy forums"

**Rules**:
- No superlatives (best, fastest, revolutionary)
- Be specific, not generic
- Mention tech if relevant ("built with X")
- Include "open-source" if applicable

---

## Data-Driven Headlines

### Formula 21: "I analyzed X and found Y"

**Structure**: `I analyzed [number] [things] and found [surprising insight]`

**Psychology**: Data = credibility, curiosity about insight

**Platform fit**: Hacker News, Medium, LinkedIn, Reddit

**Emotional trigger**: Curiosity, respect for research

**ARGUED Examples**:
- "I analyzed 500 philosophy forum discussions and found 78% die after 3 replies"
- "I analyzed 1,000 Reddit philosophy debates and found a pattern"
- "I studied 50 philosophy Discord servers and found why they fail"

**Why it works**:
- Provides value before asking for anything
- Data-driven = objective
- Creates curiosity about findings

## Word Choice Guidelines

### Power Words for Philosophy Content

**Accessibility**: Accessible, open, inclusive, everyone, free
**Quality**: Depth, nuance, structured, organized, persistent
**Community**: Discussion, debate, conversation, discourse, dialogue
**Anti-gatekeeping**: Without barriers, no paywalls, for everyone
**Persistence**: Never die, keep going, don't get lost, always there
**Facilitation**: AI-assisted, guided, structured, organized

### Words to Avoid

**On Hacker News**: Revolutionary, disruptive, best, fastest, game-changing
**On Reddit**: Promoting, check out, amazing, incredible
**Everywhere**: Superlatives without proof, corporate buzzwords
