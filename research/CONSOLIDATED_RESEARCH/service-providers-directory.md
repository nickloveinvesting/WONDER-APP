## Capacitor CI/CD & Build Services

### ⚠️ Ionic AppFlow (DISCONTINUED)
**Website:** ionic.io/appflow
**Status:** Discontinued for new customers as of 2025

**Important Note:** Ionic discontinued commercial products including AppFlow. Existing customers can use until Dec 31, 2027, but NO new signups accepted.

**Historical Pricing:**
- Basic: $499/month or $5,988/year
- ~$0.01 per Monthly Active User (MAU)

**What It Was:** Cloud-based CI/CD for Capacitor apps with native builds (no Mac required) and direct App Store publishing.

**Alternatives:** Codemagic, Capawesome Cloud, VoltBuilder

---

### Codemagic ⭐ RECOMMENDED
**Website:** codemagic.io
**What They Do:** Powerful CI/CD platform for mobile developers. Build iOS, Android, React Native, Ionic, Unity, and Flutter apps in the cloud.

**Pricing:**
- Free Tier: 500 build minutes/month
- Pay-as-you-go: Starting $0.04/month (usage-based)
- Professional: $299/month
- Fixed Annual: $3,990/year (3 concurrencies, unlimited minutes & users)
- macOS M4 Annual: $8,100/year
- Enterprise: Starting $12,000/year

**Features:**
- No Mac required for iOS builds
- Full CI/CD automation
- Official Capacitor documentation
- Multiple platform support
- Automatic builds from Git

**Turnaround Time:** Automated builds in 10-30 minutes

**App Store Submission:** Can integrate with App Store Connect

**Best For:** Teams wanting professional CI/CD pipeline

---

### VoltBuilder
**Website:** volt.build
**What They Do:** Cloud-based native app builder, positioned as AppFlow alternative.

**Pricing:**
- Free Trial: 15 days
- Premium Plans: More affordable than AppFlow (specific pricing on website)

**Features:**
- Supports all Capacitor and Cordova plugins from NPM
- Native builds without Mac
- Simple configuration

**Turnaround Time:** Builds in minutes

**App Store Submission:** Provides IPA file for manual submission

**Best For:** Individual developers or small teams

---

### Capawesome Cloud ⭐ RECOMMENDED
**Website:** cloud.capawesome.io
**What They Do:** Dedicated Capacitor Live Updates and build service, emerged as AppFlow replacement.

**Pricing:**
- Starting at $9/month
- Significantly cheaper than AppFlow

**Features:**
- Live updates (OTA)
- Capacitor-specific optimizations
- Fast deployment
- Analytics included

**Best For:** Capacitor-specific needs, especially live updates

---

### Xcode Cloud
**Website:** developer.apple.com
**What They Do:** Apple's official CI/CD service integrated with Xcode.

**Pricing:**
- Free tier with usage limits
- Pay-as-you-go beyond free tier
- Pricing based on compute time

**Features:**
- Official Apple service
- Deep Xcode integration
- Automatic App Store Connect integration
- Requires Mac-based workflow setup

**Limitations:**
- Requires Mac to set up initially
- Steeper learning curve
- Less documentation for non-native apps

**Best For:** Teams already using Mac/Xcode

## Freelance Platforms

### Upwork ⭐ RECOMMENDED
**Website:** upwork.com
**Best For:** Finding experienced iOS/Capacitor developers

**Developer Rates:**
- General iOS Developers: $16-35/hr (median $25/hr)
- Experienced iOS Developers: $35-80/hr
- Expert Capacitor Specialists: $40-100/hr

**Platform Fee:** 15% (charged to freelancer)

**Features:**
- Payment protection
- Milestone billing
- Time tracking
- Large talent pool
- Verified payment system

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Extremely low rates (<$15/hr for "experts")
- No work history
- Requests for off-platform communication
- No portfolio or vague descriptions

**Best For:** Hiring for specific tasks or short-term projects

---

### Fiverr
**Website:** fiverr.com
**Best For:** Quick, fixed-price gigs

**Typical Pricing:**
- "Convert web app to iOS" gigs: $500-2,000
- iOS Developer rates: $10-30/hr average
- App icon design: $5-200
- Screenshot design: $50-300

**Platform Fee:** 20% (charged to freelancer)

**Features:**
- Fixed-price packages
- Quick turnaround
- Gig-based service
- Portfolio previews

**Quality Considerations:**
- Higher variance in quality
- Check reviews carefully
- Start with smaller test projects
- Look for "Fiverr Pro" sellers

**Red Flags:**
- Plagiarized portfolio work
- Stock images in examples
- Unrealistic promises
- Too-good-to-be-true pricing

**Best For:** Design work, simple conversions, one-off tasks
