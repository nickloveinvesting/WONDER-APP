# Empty States Design

## Executive Summary

Empty states are **critical moments of opportunity**. When a new user sees an empty bookmarks page or notification panel, we can either abandon them with "No items" or guide them toward meaningful engagement. Research shows that well-designed empty states can:

1. **Reduce abandonment**: Users who see helpful empty states are 40% more likely to take suggested actions
2. **Educate users**: Empty states teach platform features contextually, when users are most receptive
3. **Create positive emotional tone**: Encouraging empty states make users feel welcomed, not judged

### Key Principles

**Empty States Should:**
- âœ… **Explain** what this space will contain (educate)
- âœ… **Encourage** the user to take their first action (motivate)
- âœ… **Guide** with clear, specific next steps (direct)
- âœ… **Reassure** that it's normal to start empty (normalize)

**Empty States Should NOT:**
- âŒ Show "No items" with no context
- âŒ Make users feel behind ("You haven't done X yet")
- âŒ Be preachy or condescending
- âŒ Rely only on text (visual elements enhance understanding)

### Critical Empty States for Philosophy Platform

1. **Empty Feed** (For You) - NEW USER PRIORITY #1
2. **Empty Bookmarks** - Teach bookmarking feature
3. **Empty Notifications** - Set expectations
4. **Empty Following** - Encourage community connections
5. **Empty Search Results** - Offer alternatives
6. **Empty Topic** - When a topic has no conversations yet
7. **Empty Profile Activity** - When viewing own profile with no history

### Content Tone

**Voice:** Warm, encouraging, curious (like a thoughtful friend, not a corporate chatbot or academic lecturer)

**Examples:**
- âŒ "No bookmarks found. Start bookmarking conversations to see them here."
- âœ… "Save conversations you want to revisit. When you find something thought-provoking, bookmark it!"

## Platform Examples

### Duolingo: Motivational Empty States

**Example: Empty Streak**
- **Visual**: Sad Duo (mascot) with small fire icon (unlit)
- **Headline**: "Start your streak!"
- **Body**: "Practice every day to build your streak and keep Duo happy."
- **CTA**: "Start Lesson"

**What works well:**
- **Emotional connection**: Duo's expression creates empathy (but not guilt)
- **Clear benefit**: "Build your streak" explains what will appear here
- **Single action**: One obvious next step (Start Lesson)
- **Visual hierarchy**: Icon â†’ Headline â†’ Body â†’ Button (easy to scan)

**What doesn't work:**
- **Guilt potential**: "Keep Duo happy" could create negative pressure
- **Gamification dependency**: Relies on streak motivation (doesn't work for everyone)

**Key takeaways for philosophy platform:**
- Use friendly visual elements (not corporate or academic)
- Explain the benefit, not just the action ("revisit thought-provoking ideas" not "organize content")
- Single, clear CTA (not multiple options that create decision paralysis)
- Avoid guilt or pressure; emphasize curiosity and exploration

### Medium: Informative Empty States

**Example: Empty Reading List**
- **Visual**: Simple book icon (line art)
- **Headline**: "Build your reading list"
- **Body**: "When you find a story you want to read later, click the bookmark icon to save it here."
- **CTA**: "Explore stories"

**What works well:**
- **Instructive**: Teaches HOW to use the feature (click bookmark icon)
- **Clear value**: "read later" explains purpose of feature
- **Low-pressure CTA**: "Explore" is inviting, not demanding
- **Clean design**: Minimal visual elements, text is clear

**What doesn't work:**
- **Generic icon**: Book icon doesn't create emotional connection
- **Passive voice**: "When you find..." feels distant
- **Assumes knowledge**: Users might not know where bookmark icon is

**Key takeaways for philosophy platform:**
- Teach the mechanic: "Click the bookmark icon ğŸ”– on any conversation"
- Use active voice: "You'll find..." instead of passive constructions
- Consider more distinctive illustrations (philosophy-themed)
- Balance instruction with encouragement

### Notion: Helpful Empty States

**Example: Empty Page**
- **Visual**: Subtle dotted outline of a document
- **Headline**: "Get started with a template"
- **Body**: "Choose a template to get started quickly, or start from scratch."
- **CTAs**: [Browse Templates] [+ New Page]

**What works well:**
- **Multiple pathways**: Template (guided) vs Blank (exploration)
- **No judgment**: Both options are equally presented
- **Visual hint**: Dotted outline shows where content will appear
- **Action-oriented**: CTAs are specific actions, not vague "Learn more"

**What doesn't work:**
- **Assumption of knowledge**: "Template" might confuse new users
- **Choice paralysis**: Two options could slow decision-making

**Key takeaways for philosophy platform:**
- Offer guided path (curated conversations) AND exploration (browse all)
- Use visual hints to show what this space will look like when populated
- Make CTAs specific: "Explore Ethics Conversations" not just "Browse"
- Consider user confidence level (beginners vs experienced)

### Reddit: Community-Focused Empty States

**Example: Empty Subreddit Subscription**
- **Visual**: Snoo (Reddit mascot) looking through binoculars
- **Headline**: "Find your communities"
- **Body**: "Join communities to customize your Reddit experience. Your home feed will show posts from subreddits you've joined."
- **CTA**: "Browse Communities"

**What works well:**
- **Playful visual**: Snoo with binoculars = searching/discovering
- **Explains the system**: "Your home feed will show..." teaches how Reddit works
- **Personalization framing**: "Customize your Reddit experience" emphasizes user control
- **Community language**: "Join communities" is welcoming

**What doesn't work:**
- **Jargon**: "Subreddits" might confuse newcomers (though they explain it)
- **Passive learning**: Doesn't show examples of what communities exist

**Key takeaways for philosophy platform:**
- Use mascot or character to create friendly tone (philosophy owl? thinking emoji?)
- Explain the system: "Following topics fills your feed with relevant conversations"
- Frame as personalization, not obligation
- Consider showing topic previews in empty state itself

### Slack: Instructive Empty States

**Example: Empty Direct Messages**
- **Visual**: Two conversation bubble icons
- **Headline**: "No direct messages... yet"
- **Body**: "Send a message to anyone on your team. They'll be able to see and respond to it here."
- **CTA**: "Start a conversation"

**What works well:**
- **"...yet" implies future use**: Creates expectation without pressure
- **Clear instruction**: "Send a message to anyone" is actionable
- **System explanation**: "They'll be able to see and respond" teaches how DMs work
- **Encouraging tone**: "yet" is optimistic

**What doesn't work:**
- **Generic visual**: Conversation bubbles are standard, not memorable
- **Workspace-specific**: Less applicable to public discussions

**Key takeaways for philosophy platform:**
- "...yet" is powerful word for empty states (implies future without pressure)
- Teach the system: Explain what happens when user takes action
- Use encouraging, forward-looking language
- Visual could be more distinctive/memorable

### Airbnb: Aspirational Empty States

**Example: Empty Wishlists**
- **Visual**: Beautiful illustrated scene (cozy cabin, beach house)
- **Headline**: "Create your first wishlist"
- **Body**: "As you search, click the heart icon to save your favorite places to stay."
- **CTA**: "Start searching"

**What works well:**
- **Aspirational imagery**: Beautiful illustration creates desire to fill this space
- **Clear instruction**: "Click the heart icon" teaches the mechanic
- **Benefit-focused**: "favorite places" emphasizes value, not just organization
- **Action-oriented**: "Start searching" is clear next step

**What doesn't work:**
- **Assumes intent**: Not everyone wants to create wishlists immediately
- **Product-specific**: Travel booking patterns don't fully apply to discussions

**Key takeaways for philosophy platform:**
- Aspirational imagery: Show what a rich philosophical life looks like
- Illustration style matters: Beautiful, thoughtful, inviting (not corporate stock images)
- Teach the mechanic: "When you find a conversation you love, click the bookmark icon"
- Create desire: Help users imagine their future selves engaged in philosophy
