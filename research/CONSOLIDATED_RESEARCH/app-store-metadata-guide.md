## App Name and Subtitle

### App Name

**Character Limit**: 30 characters (strictly enforced)

**Best Practices:**
- **Be descriptive**: Name should hint at app function
- **Include primary keyword**: Can boost ranking by 10%+
- **Keep it memorable**: Easy to spell and pronounce
- **Avoid special characters**: No ‚Ñ¢ unless you own the trademark
- **Check availability**: Search App Store before committing

**Formula Options:**

1. **Brand Name Only**
   - Example: "Philosophy" (11 chars)
   - Pros: Clean, memorable, brandable
   - Cons: Requires marketing to explain function

2. **Brand + Function**
   - Example: "Philosophy - Deep Debates" (25 chars)
   - Pros: Clear purpose, keyword included
   - Cons: Uses more characters

3. **Function + Brand**
   - Example: "Deep Debates: Philosophy" (25 chars)
   - Pros: Function-first for discovery
   - Cons: Less brand-forward

**What NOT to Do:**
- ‚ùå Keyword stuffing: "Philosophy Debate Chat Social Network Discussion Forum"
- ‚ùå Special characters: "Philosophy‚Ñ¢ - The Best App!"
- ‚ùå Emojis: "Philosophy üß† Deep Thinking"
- ‚ùå Competitor names: "Philosophy - Like Reddit"
- ‚ùå Pricing info: "Philosophy - Free Chat"

**How to Check Availability:**
1. Search exact name in App Store
2. If exists, you must differentiate
3. Apple may reject if too similar to existing app

**For Philosophy App Examples:**
- "Philosophy" (simple)
- "Philosophy - Deep Debates"
- "Philosophy: Think & Debate"
- "Dialectic - Philosophy App"

### Subtitle

**Character Limit**: 30 characters (strictly enforced)

**Purpose**: Appears below app name in search results and product page

**Best Practices:**
- **Value proposition**: What user gets
- **Clarity over cleverness**: Be direct
- **Include secondary keyword**: Different from app name
- **Action-oriented**: Verbs that describe activity
- **No repetition**: Don't repeat app name

**Formula Options:**

1. **Action + Outcome**
   - "Debate Ideas, Sharpen Mind"
   - "Think Deeper, Grow Smarter"

2. **Feature + Benefit**
   - "Socratic Dialogue Daily"
   - "Philosophical Conversations"

3. **Audience + Promise**
   - "For Deep Thinkers"
   - "Where Ideas Meet Logic"

**For Philosophy App Examples:**
- "Engage in Deep Debates" (23 chars)
- "Think Deeper Together" (21 chars)
- "Philosophical Conversations" (27 chars)
- "Debate Ideas, Find Truth" (24 chars)

**What NOT to Do:**
- ‚ùå Generic: "The Best App for You"
- ‚ùå Keyword list: "Debate, Chat, Philosophy, Talk"
- ‚ùå Redundant: "Philosophy App for Philosophy"
- ‚ùå Salesy: "Download Now for Free!"

## App Icon Design

### Technical Requirements

**Mandatory Specifications:**
- **Size**: 1024 x 1024 pixels
- **Format**: PNG
- **Color space**: RGB
- **Resolution**: 72 DPI
- **Shape**: Square (no rounded corners - Apple adds these)
- **Transparency**: NOT allowed
- **Alpha channels**: NOT allowed
- **Background**: Must be opaque

**Apple Auto-Scaling:**
- Apple automatically scales your 1024x1024 icon to all required sizes
- Sizes include: 180x180, 120x120, 87x87, 80x80, 76x76, 60x60, 58x58, 40x40, 29x29

### Design Best Practices

**1. Simplicity**
- **Single focal point**: One clear visual element
- **Minimal text**: Avoid text unless essential to brand
- **Bold shapes**: Recognizable even at small sizes
- **Limited colors**: 2-4 colors maximum

**2. Recognition**
- **Unique**: Stands out from competitors
- **Memorable**: Instantly recognizable
- **Scalable**: Looks good from 29x29 to 1024x1024
- **Timeless**: Avoid trendy design that dates quickly

**3. Platform Consistency**
- **iOS style**: Follow Apple's Human Interface Guidelines
- **Avoid shadows**: iOS adds depth effect
- **Fill canvas**: Use full 1024x1024 space
- **Test on backgrounds**: Check on both light and dark modes

**Design Process:**

**Step 1: Concept** (2-4 hours)
- Brainstorm visual metaphors for your app
- Philosophy app examples:
  - Thought bubble
  - Yin-yang (dialectic)
  - Speech bubbles crossing
  - Light bulb
  - Ancient Greek column
  - Open book
  - Owl (wisdom)

**Step 2: Sketch** (1-2 hours)
- Create 10-20 rough sketches
- Test at small size (print at 1 inch square)
- Get feedback from 5+ people

**Step 3: Digital Design** (4-8 hours)
- Use Figma, Adobe Illustrator, or Sketch
- Create in vector for perfect scaling
- Export at 1024x1024
- Test on different backgrounds
- Test at multiple sizes

**Step 4: Validate** (1-2 hours)
- View on actual iPhone home screen
- Check in App Store search results
- Compare to competitor icons
- A/B test with target users if possible

**Design Tools:**
- **Free**: Figma (free tier), Canva
- **Paid**: Adobe Illustrator, Sketch, Affinity Designer
- **Services**: 99designs, Fiverr, Upwork (hire designer)

**Common Mistakes:**
- ‚ùå Too complex: Unrecognizable at small sizes
- ‚ùå Too much text: Illegible when scaled down
- ‚ùå Gradients: Can look muddy at small sizes
- ‚ùå Thin lines: Disappear at small sizes
- ‚ùå Low contrast: Doesn't pop in App Store
- ‚ùå Generic: Looks like every other app

**Testing Your Icon:**
1. Export at 1024x1024
2. Scale down to 60x60 in image editor
3. If you can't recognize it, redesign
4. Place next to competitor icons
5. Ask: "Does it stand out?"

- **Color space**: RGB
- **Resolution**: 72 DPI
- **Transparency**: Not allowed
- **Quantity**: 1-10 screenshots per device size
- **Order matters**: First 3 shown in search results

### Screenshot Strategy

#### The First 3 Rule

**Critical**: First 3 screenshots are visible in search results WITHOUT clicking

**First Screenshot Goals:**
1. **Instant understanding**: What is this app?
2. **Value proposition**: Why download?
3. **Visual appeal**: Make them want to see more

**Strategy:**
- Screenshot 1: Hero feature or main screen
- Screenshot 2: Key differentiator
- Screenshot 3: Social proof or secondary feature

#### Screenshot Types

**1. Pure Screenshots**
- Direct captures from app
- **Pros**: Fastest, most authentic
- **Cons**: May not highlight features well
- **Best for**: Simple, visually stunning apps

**2. Screenshots with Text Overlays** (Recommended)
- Screenshots + marketing copy
- **Pros**: Explains features clearly, higher conversion
- **Cons**: More design work required
- **Best for**: Most apps, especially new concepts

**3. Device Frames**
- Screenshots inside iPhone mockup
- **Pros**: Professional, contextual
- **Cons**: Wastes space, less room for content
- **Best for**: B2B apps, professional tools

### Content Strategy for Philosophy App

**Screenshot 1: Main Debate View**
- Show active philosophical debate
- Text overlay: "Engage in Deep Philosophical Debates"
- Highlight: Conversation bubbles, quality discussions

**Screenshot 2: Socratic Method Feature**
- Show AI-powered Socratic questioning
- Text overlay: "Sharpen Your Thinking with Socratic AI"
- Highlight: Intelligent questioning in action

**Screenshot 3: Community**
- Show diverse perspectives
- Text overlay: "Join Thinkers from Around the World"
- Social proof: "50,000+ Deep Thinkers" (when applicable)

**Screenshot 4: Daily Challenges**
- Show daily philosophical question
- Text overlay: "Daily Philosophy Challenges"
- Highlight: Streak counter, gamification

**Screenshot 5: Learning Progress**
- Show knowledge graph or progress
- Text overlay: "Track Your Philosophical Growth"
- Highlight: Achievements, learning paths

**Screenshot 6-10: Additional Features**
- Reading recommendations
- Argument mapping
- Community highlights
- Premium features
- Safety/moderation features

### Design Best Practices

**Text Overlays:**
- **Font size**: Large enough to read on phone (min 40pt)
- **Contrast**: Text readable on any background
- **Positioning**: Top third (most visible)
- **Brevity**: 3-7 words per screenshot
- **Hierarchy**: Feature title + brief benefit

**Visual Hierarchy:**
1. Feature title (largest text)
2. Screenshot content (central focus)
3. Benefit description (smaller supporting text)
4. Optional: Icon or visual element

**Color Psychology:**
- Blue: Trust, intelligence, calm
- Purple: Wisdom, creativity, luxury
- Green: Growth, harmony, learning
- Orange: Energy, enthusiasm, friendly
- Avoid: Red (aggressive), Pink (not serious)

**Remove:**
- Status bar (time, battery, signal)
- Placeholder content ("Lorem ipsum")
- Personal information
- Copyrighted content
- Inappropriate content (remember: 4+ rating for screenshots)

### Tools for Creating Screenshots

**Capture Tools:**
- iOS Simulator (Xcode) - free, perfect sizes
- Physical device + screenshot - may need resizing
- Design tools (Figma, Sketch) - full control

**Design Tools:**
- **Figma** (free tier available)
  - Templates: Search "App Store screenshots"
  - Plugins: Screenshot, Device Mockups

- **Screenshot Designer Tools**:
  - AppLaunchpad Screenshot Builder
  - Previewed.app
  - Shotsnapp
  - AppFollow Screenshot Builder

- **Template Sources**:
  - Figma Community (free templates)
  - Creative Market
  - Envato Elements

**Design Process:**

**Step 1: Capture** (1 hour)
- Take screenshots of all key screens
- Use consistent data (not "Lorem ipsum")
- Remove status bars in iOS Settings > Screenshot

**Step 2: Select** (30 minutes)
- Choose 6-10 best screens
- Prioritize user value over technical features
- Consider user journey/story flow

**Step 3: Design** (4-8 hours)
- Create template in Figma/Photoshop
- Add backgrounds, device frames (optional)
- Add text overlays explaining each feature
- Ensure consistent style across all screenshots
- Export at required dimensions

**Step 4: Validate** (1 hour)
- View on actual iPhone
- Ask: "Do I understand what this app does?"
- Test with people unfamiliar with app
- A/B test if possible

## Keywords and ASO

### Understanding App Store Keywords

**What They Do:**
- Help App Store understand your app
- Determine where you appear in search results
- Critical for organic discovery

**Character Limit**: 100 characters total (strictly enforced)

**How It Works:**
- Apple combines your keywords into different combinations
- Each word matches with other words
- "philosophy debate" + "deep thinking" = matches "philosophy thinking", "deep debate", etc.

### Keyword Research Process

**Step 1: Brainstorm** (30 minutes)
List all relevant terms:
- What your app does
- Who it's for
- Problems it solves
- Features it has
- Alternatives/competitors

**Step 2: Research Tools** (2-3 hours)

**Free Tools:**
- App Store search suggestions (type partial keywords)
- Google Trends (compare keyword popularity)
- Competitor app keywords (analyze their metadata)

**Paid Tools:**
- AppTweak
- Sensor Tower
- App Annie (now data.ai)
- SearchAds Intelligence

**Metrics to Check:**
- **Search volume**: How many people search this?
- **Difficulty**: How competitive is ranking?
- **Relevance**: Does it match your app?

**Sweet Spot**: High volume + low difficulty + high relevance

**Step 3: Prioritize** (1 hour)

**Score each keyword:**
- Relevance (0-10): How well does it describe your app?
- Volume (0-10): How many people search it?
- Difficulty (0-10): How hard to rank? (inverse - 0 = very hard)
- Total score = Relevance + Volume + (10 - Difficulty)

**Choose top keywords that fit in 100 characters**

### Keyword Strategy

**What to Include:**
- Core functionality keywords
- Use case keywords
- Problem keywords
- Category keywords (if not in app name)
- Alternative app names (if not copyrighted)

**What to EXCLUDE:**
- Your app name (already indexed)
- Category names (already indexed from category selection)
- Competitor names (violates Apple policy)
- Irrelevant keywords
- Duplicate words

**Formatting Rules:**
- Comma-separated
- No spaces after commas (saves characters!)
- Lowercase only (Apple ignores case)
- Singular form only (Apple indexes plurals automatically)
- No special characters or symbols

**Analysis:**

- Don't include "metaphysics", "epistemology" (too specific, low volume)
- Don't include "app" (obvious, wasted characters)

### Monitoring and Iteration

**Track Rankings:**
- Monitor keyword rankings weekly
- Track downloads from keywords
- Identify which keywords drive installs

**Update Strategy:**

## Categories and Age Rating

### Primary and Secondary Categories

**Primary Category** (Required)
- Most important for discovery
- Choose the single best fit
- Cannot change without app update

**Secondary Category** (Optional but Recommended)
- Additional discovery opportunity
- Complements primary category
- 15-20% more visibility

**For Philosophy App:**

**Option 1:**
- Primary: Education
- Secondary: Social Networking

**Option 2:**
- Primary: Social Networking
- Secondary: Education

**Consider:**
- Where is your target audience browsing?
- Which category has less competition?
- Which category best describes core value?

**Popular Categories:**
- Education
- Social Networking
- Books
- News
- Lifestyle
- Reference
- Entertainment

### Age Rating

**Process:**
- Answer questionnaire in App Store Connect
- Apple assigns rating automatically
- Based on content frequency

**Rating Levels:**
- 4+: No objectionable content
- 9+: Mild or infrequent content
- 13+: Moderate content
- 16+: Frequent mature content (NEW in 2025)
- 18+: Restricted content

**Content Types Evaluated:**
- Cartoon/Fantasy Violence
- Realistic Violence
- Sexual Content or Nudity
- Profanity or Crude Humor
- Alcohol, Tobacco, or Drug Use
- Mature/Suggestive Themes
- Simulated Gambling
- Horror/Fear Themes
- Medical/Treatment Information
- Unrestricted Web Access
- User-Generated Content

**For Philosophy App:**

**Likely Rating: 13+** due to:
- User-generated content (discussions)
- Mature/complex themes (ethics, morality)
- Unrestricted web access (if applicable)

**Important:**
- Answer honestly (mis-rating can get app rejected)
- Consider ALL content, including UGC
- Consider AI-generated content
- **Metadata must be 4+ appropriate** regardless of app rating

**Deadline:** All apps must have updated age ratings by January 31, 2026

## Localization Strategy

### Why Localize?

**Impact:**
- 50%+ more downloads in localized markets
- Higher conversion rates
- Better keyword ranking in local languages
- Access to underserved markets

**Investment:**
- Time: 2-5 hours per language for metadata
- Cost: $50-200 per language for professional translation
- Ongoing: Update with each metadata change

### What to Localize

**Metadata:**
- App name (can differ per locale)
- Subtitle
- Description
- Keywords (100 chars per locale)
- Screenshots (optional but recommended)
- App preview videos (optional)
- What's New text

**In-App Content:**
- UI text
- Error messages
- Notifications
- Onboarding

### Priority Languages

**Tier 1: Largest Markets**
- English (US) - largest market
- Chinese Simplified - second largest
- Japanese - high-value users
- German - strong EU market
- French - global reach
- Spanish - Latin America + Spain

**Tier 2: Strategic Markets**
- Korean
- Portuguese (Brazil)
- Russian
- Italian
- Dutch
- Swedish

**For Philosophy App:**

**Start with:** English (US)

**Add next:** Spanish, German, French
- Large educated audiences
- Interest in philosophy
- Lower translation costs (Roman alphabet)

**Later:** Japanese, Chinese, Korean
- High app usage
- Different character sets require UI updates

### Translation Best Practices

**Option 1: DIY + Native Speaker Review**
- Translate yourself (if fluent)
- Have native speaker review
- Cost: Low
- Quality: Varies

**Option 2: Professional Translation Service**
- Gengo, Rev, One Hour Translation
- Cost: $0.05-0.10 per word
- Quality: Good
- Turnaround: 1-3 days

**Option 3: Localization Platform**
- Lokalise, Phrase, Crowdin
- Cost: $50-200/month
- Quality: Good
- Features: Translation memory, context, collaboration

**What NOT to Do:**
- ‚ùå Google Translate only (inaccurate, unnatural)
- ‚ùå Translate keywords literally (research local search terms)
- ‚ùå Ignore cultural differences
- ‚ùå Use inconsistent terminology

### Screenshots Localization

**Options:**

**1. No Localization** (Quickest)
- Keep English screenshots for all locales
- Still better than no localization

**2. Text Overlay Localization** (Recommended)
- Translate marketing text on screenshots
- Keep UI in English
- Cost-effective compromise

**3. Full Localization** (Best)
- Translate all on-screen text
- May require app localization first
- Highest conversion but most expensive
