# ARGUED Engagement Playbook
## How to Engage Authentically and Convert Interest to Community

---

## Core Engagement Philosophy

**Principle**: Every comment is an opportunity to build relationship, not just defend product.

**Goals**:
1. Convert curious visitors to engaged users
2. Build trust through authentic, thoughtful responses
3. Create advocates who spread word organically
4. Learn from feedback (positive and negative)
5. Establish community culture from day one

**Not Goals**:
- Win every argument
- Defend every criticism
- Convert every visitor
- Please everyone

---

## Response Time Targets

### Launch Day (Critical)
**Platform**: All
**Response Time**: 5-15 minutes
**Rationale**: Algorithm boost, shows you care, builds momentum
**Energy Level**: Maximum - this is a sprint

### Week 1 (High Priority)
**Platform**: Product Hunt, Hacker News, Reddit
**Response Time**: 30 minutes - 1 hour
**Rationale**: Still in critical visibility window
**Energy Level**: High - sustained effort

### Week 2-4 (Standard)
**Platform**: All
**Response Time**: 1-3 hours (same day)
**Rationale**: Building relationships, less algorithm dependency
**Energy Level**: Medium - marathon pace

### Month 2+ (Sustainable)
**Platform**: All
**Response Time**: Same day (2-4 hours)
**Rationale**: Sustainable long-term engagement
**Energy Level**: Sustainable pace

### Special Cases
**Influencer/Press**: Within 30 minutes, any time
**Bug Report**: Immediately
**Angry User**: Within 1 hour
**Praise**: Within 30 minutes (reinforce positive)

## How to Handle Common Objections

### Objection 1: "This Will Never Scale"

**Weak Response**:
"Yes it will! We have great plans for scaling!"

**Strong Response**:
```
You might be right. Lots of quality-focused communities struggle at scale.

We're optimizing for quality over growth. If we stay at 10,000 deeply engaged
users instead of 10M passive ones, that's okay with us.

That said, we think some things scale: good incentives, clear culture,
distributed moderation. Time will tell.

What do you think are the hardest parts of scaling discourse quality?
```

**Why Better**:
- Acknowledges real concern
- Reframes success (quality > quantity)
- Shows thoughtful approach
- Learns from objection

### Objection 2: "I Don't Have Time for Long Debates"

**Weak Response**:
"It doesn't take that long!"

**Strong Response**:
```
Totally fair - not everyone wants to spend hours on a single debate thread.

ARGUED is definitely optimized for depth over speed. If you prefer quick
back-and-forth, Twitter or Reddit might be better fits.

We're building for people who enjoy the occasional deep dive. Like how some
people love 3-hour podcast episodes even if they don't always have time.

Not for everyone, and that's okay!
```

**Why Better**:
- Validates concern
- Clear about who it's for
- Okay with not being for everyone
- Good analogy

### Objection 3: "People Don't Change Their Minds"

**Weak Response**:
"Yes they do! It happens all the time!"

**Strong Response**:
```
In public, rarely. You're right.

But in our beta, we've seen it more than I expected. Not every debate, but
enough to be interesting.

Hypothesis: People change minds more when:
1. They're not performing for an audience
2. Changing mind is rewarded, not mocked
3. Structure forces engaging with strongest counterarguments

Still early, might be wrong. But the beta has been surprisingly promising.

Have you ever changed your mind in an online discussion? What made that
possible?
```

**Why Better**:
- Agrees with observation
- Shares real data
- Explains hypothesis
- Humble about outcomes
- Invites their experience

### Objection 4: "Just Use [Competitor X]"

**Weak Response**:
"No, ours is better because..."

**Strong Response**:
```
[Competitor X] is great for [what they do well]!

ARGUED is different in [specific way]. Whether that's "better" depends on
what you're looking for.

If you want [use case], definitely use [Competitor X].
If you want [our use case], ARGUED might be worth trying.

Have you used [Competitor X]? What do you like/not like about it?
```

**Why Better**:
- Respects competitor
- Clear differentiation
- Okay with not being universal solution
- Learns about user needs

### Objection 5: "This Is Impossible / Has Never Worked"

**Weak Response**:
"We're different! We'll make it work!"

**Strong Response**:
```
You're not wrong - history is littered with failed attempts at "better discourse."

We might fail too. Probably will, if we're being honest about base rates.

But we think it's worth trying because [reason]. And we're learning a lot

What do you think was the core failure mode of previous attempts?
```

**Why Better**:
- Acknowledges history

- Values attempt even if outcome uncertain

## Converting Interest to Signups

### The Funnel
1. **Awareness**: They see your post
2. **Interest**: They read/comment
3. **Consideration**: They think about trying
4. **Action**: They sign up
5. **Activation**: They actually use it

### Strategies by Stage

#### Stage 1â†’2: Awareness to Interest
**Goal**: Make them want to learn more

**Tactics**:
- Compelling title/hook
- Interesting screenshot/example
- Curiosity gap ("How we got people to change minds...")
- Relatable problem statement

#### Stage 2â†’3: Interest to Consideration
**Goal**: Make them think "This could be for me"

**Tactics**:
- Show, don't tell (link to actual good debates)
- Use cases they can imagine themselves in
- Reduce perceived risk ("Free, no signup needed to browse")
- Social proof ("Our beta testers said...")

#### Stage 3â†’4: Consideration to Action
**Goal**: Remove friction to trying

**Tactics**:
- Clear call to action (but not pushy)
- Low commitment ("Check it out" not "Sign up today!")
- Make signup easy (email only, no credit card)
- Provide direct link in convenient place

#### Stage 4â†’5: Action to Activation
**Goal**: Get them to first value moment

**Tactics**:
- Great onboarding
- Highlight active debates
- Suggest relevant topics
- Personal welcome (if small enough)
- Quick win (easy first action)

**When You Have < 100 Users**:
- Personal welcome message to every signup
