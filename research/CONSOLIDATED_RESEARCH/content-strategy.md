### **Medium Engagement** (5-15 minutes)

#### **Philosophy Explained (ELI5 Style)**
**Format**: 500-word explainer + examples + discussion prompts

**Topics**:
- "What is existentialism? (And why does it matter?)"
- "The trolley problem: A beginner's guide"
- "Kant's categorical imperative, explained"
- "Free will vs. determinism: What's the debate?"

**Structure**:
```
1. Hook (relatable intro)
2. Core concept (simple language)
3. Real-world example
4. Philosophical debate (different views)
5. Discussion prompt (engage users)
```

**Why It Works**:
- Removes intimidation factor
- Builds philosophical literacy
- Evergreen content (re-shareable)
- SEO-friendly (Google searches)

**Production**:
- 2-3 per week
- Mix of classic topics + contemporary issues
- Community voting on next topics
- Expert review for accuracy

**Metrics**:
- Reading completion rate
- Bookmarks/saves
- Discussion participation
- Click-through to related debates

### **Deep Engagement** (30-60+ minutes)

#### **Long-Form Debates** (Written or Video)
**Format**: Two philosophers, opposing views, moderated discussion

**Topics**:
- "Is free will real? Libertarianism vs. Hard Determinism"
- "What is justice? Rawls vs. Nozick"
- "Does God exist? Theist vs. Atheist debate"

**Structure**:
```
1. Opening statements (10 min each)
2. Rebuttals (7 min each)
3. Cross-examination (10 min)
4. Closing arguments (5 min each)
5. Community Q&A
```

**Why It Works**:
- Deep intellectual engagement
- Models good philosophical discourse
- Educational (learn arguments from both sides)
- Evergreen (remains valuable long-term)

**Production**:
- 1-2 per month (high production value)
- Invite philosophy PhDs or top community members
- Professional editing (if video)

**Metrics**:
- Watch/read completion
- Argument quality ratings
- Discussion spawned
- Citations in future debates

## 5. COMBATING SHORT-FORM SHALLOW CONTENT

### **The TikTok/Twitter Problem**

**Challenge**: Platforms reward:
- Brevity (280 characters, 60-second videos)
- Clickbait (provocative > accurate)
- Engagement (conflict > nuance)
- Volume (post daily > occasional deep work)

**Result**: Philosophy reduced to soundbites, nuance lost

### **PhiloDuel's Solution: Layered Content**

#### **Layer 1: The Hook** (30 seconds)
Philosophical hot take or meme
- **Goal**: Capture attention
- **Metric**: Stops scrolling

#### **Layer 2: The Context** (3-5 minutes)
Short explainer or thought experiment
- **Goal**: Build understanding
- **Metric**: Clicks "Learn more"

#### **Layer 3: The Depth** (15-30 minutes)
Full debate or essay
- **Goal**: Genuine philosophical engagement
- **Metric**: Completes reading/debate

#### **Layer 4: The Mastery** (1+ hours)
Research, tournaments, original work
- **Goal**: Expertise development
- **Metric**: Creates content, teaches others

**Key Principle**: Shallow content is the gateway, not the destination

---

### **Intentional Friction**

**Problem**: Infinite scroll = mindless consumption

**Solutions**:
- âœ… "You've been scrolling for 10 minutes. Start a debate?"
- âœ… Daily view limits on hot takes (nudge toward depth)
- âœ… "Hot Take Break" (forced cooldown after 20 quick views)
- âœ… Recommendation algorithm favors depth (not just engagement)

**Goal**: Steer users from shallow â†’ deep content
