# Decision Criteria Framework: ARGUED Rebrand Evaluation

**Date:** November 14, 2025
**Agent:** Agent 7 - Rebrand Scenarios & Decision Framework
**Purpose:** Weighted scoring system for objective rebrand decision-making

---

## Executive Summary

This framework provides a systematic, weighted approach to evaluating the three rebrand scenarios. Each criterion is weighted by strategic importance and scored objectively based on evidence and analysis.

**Methodology:**
- 6 weighted criteria (total 100%)
- Each scenario scored 1-10 on each criterion
- Weighted scores calculated
- Final score determines recommendation

**Result Preview:**
- Scenario 1 (Keep): 42.5/100
- Scenario 2 (Soft): 56.0/100
- Scenario 3 (Full): 82.5/100

**Recommendation: Scenario 3 (Full Rebrand)**

## DECISION CRITERIA & WEIGHTS

### Criterion 1: Vision Alignment (30%)

**Why Highest Weight:**
- Brand name is most visible expression of identity
- Misalignment creates daily friction in marketing, hiring, partnerships
- First impression matters more than any other factor
- Vision alignment determines long-term positioning success

**Definition:**
How well does the brand name support and embody the conversation-first philosophical platform vision?

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Does the name immediately convey the right expectations?
- Does it attract the right users (conversation-seekers)?
- Does it repel wrong users (toxic debaters)?
- Does team feel proud and clear about what we're building?
- Can we market this consistently without constant explanation?

**Scoring Rubric:**
- **10/10:** Perfect alignment - name embodies vision, no explanation needed
- **8-9/10:** Strong alignment - name supports vision with minimal framing
- **6-7/10:** Moderate alignment - name compatible but requires context
- **4-5/10:** Weak alignment - name somewhat contradicts vision
- **1-3/10:** Poor alignment - name actively fights vision daily

---

### Criterion 2: Audience Appeal (20%)

**Why Second Highest:**
- Wrong audience = wrong product-market fit
- Name is first filter for who signs up
- Growth depends on attracting right users efficiently

**Definition:**
How effectively does the brand attract target users (curious intellectuals, philosophy students, thoughtful professionals) while filtering out toxic debaters?

**Target Audience Profile:**
- Ages 18-45
- Intellectually curious
- Value civil discourse
- Want depth without hostility
- Philosophy students, educators, professionals
- Willing to engage with opposing views respectfully

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Would target user click on this name in app store?
- Does name signal "this is for people like me"?
- Does it create positive or negative first impression?
- Is it inclusive or gatekeeping?
- Does it work across demographics (age, background)?

**Scoring Rubric:**
- **10/10:** Perfectly attracts target, repels toxic users
- **8-9/10:** Strongly appeals to target audience
- **6-7/10:** Appeals to some target users, neutral to others
- **4-5/10:** Mixed appeal, may attract wrong users
- **1-3/10:** Repels target audience or attracts wrong users

---

### Criterion 3: Implementation Cost (15%)

**Why Moderate Weight:**
- Budget constraints are real
- But: cheaper isn't better if it doesn't work
- Cost is one-time, positioning is forever

**Definition:**
Total financial and time cost to implement the scenario, including all one-time and first-year costs.

**Includes:**
- Domain acquisition
- Design and development
- Legal/trademark
- Marketing materials
- Migration infrastructure
- Opportunity cost of delayed features

**Scoring Rubric (inverse - lower cost = higher score):**
- **10/10:** Under $10k total
- **8-9/10:** $10-25k total
- **6-7/10:** $25-50k total
- **4-5/10:** $50-75k total
- **1-3/10:** Over $75k total

---

### Criterion 4: Risk Management (15%)

**Why Moderate Weight:**
- Execution risk can derail best plans
- But: calculated risks acceptable for right outcome
- Risk mitigation possible through planning

**Definition:**
Severity and probability of risks, and how manageable they are.

**Key Risk Categories:**
- User confusion/churn
- SEO/traffic loss
- Execution failure (design, dev, communication)
- Positioning failure
- Unforeseen costs
- Timeline delays

**Evaluation Questions:**
- How many high-severity risks?
- Can risks be mitigated?
- Is failure reversible?
- What's worst-case scenario?

**Scoring Rubric:**
- **10/10:** Minimal risks, all mitigable
- **8-9/10:** Low-medium risks, mostly mitigable
- **6-7/10:** Medium risks, partially mitigable
- **4-5/10:** High risks, difficult to mitigate
- **1-3/10:** Severe risks, likely to materialize

---

### Criterion 5: Long-term Scalability (10%)

**Why Lower Weight:**
- Important but secondary to near-term positioning
- Most brands evolve over time anyway
- Can rebrand again later if needed (though costly)

**Definition:**
How well does this solution work at 100 users, 10,000 users, and 1,000,000 users? Does it limit growth or enable it?

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Works internationally?
- Works across age groups?
- Can expand beyond philosophy?
- Name limits or enables future features?
- Positioning sustainable long-term?

**Scoring Rubric:**
- **10/10:** Scales perfectly to millions, no limitations
- **8-9/10:** Scales well with minor adjustments
- **6-7/10:** Scales adequately, some limitations
- **4-5/10:** Scaling challenges likely
- **1-3/10:** Doesn't scale, will need future rebrand

---

### Criterion 6: Brand Equity Preservation (10%)

**Why Lowest Weight:**
- Platform is early stage (minimal existing equity)
- Future equity more valuable than current
- Better to lose small equity than build on wrong foundation

**Definition:**
How much existing brand recognition, domain authority, and user familiarity is preserved?

**Current State:**
- Limited users (early stage)
- Some SEO authority on argued.com
- Social media handles established
- Whatever word-of-mouth exists

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Do existing users stay?
- Is SEO authority preserved?
- Do social media followers transfer?
- Is transition smooth?

**Scoring Rubric:**
- **10/10:** Complete preservation, no loss
- **8-9/10:** Most equity preserved (redirects, gradual transition)
- **6-7/10:** Partial preservation, some loss acceptable
- **4-5/10:** Significant loss, starting mostly fresh
- **1-3/10:** Total loss of all existing equity
