# Audience Research: Philosophy Conversation Platform

## Research Objective

Before we can effectively market our philosophical conversation platform, we need to deeply understand our target users. This research aims to answer critical questions about WHO will use our platform, WHY they'll use it, WHERE they currently hang out online, and HOW to speak to them.

## Current Unknowns

- **Who exactly is our target user?** (Demographics, psychographics, behaviors)
- **What problems keep them up at night?**
- **Where do they currently have philosophical discussions?**
- **What language resonates with them?**
- **What triggers them to try new platforms?**
- **How do they make decisions about joining communities?**

## Research Methodology

This research employs 8 parallel research streams, each focused on a specific aspect of audience understanding:

### Agent 1: Reddit Philosophy Community User Analysis
**Mission:** Deep dive into WHO actually participates in philosophy subreddits

**Research Focus:**
- Subreddit demographics analysis (r/philosophy, r/askphilosophy, r/Stoicism, r/existentialism)
- User archetypes in philosophy communities
- Posting patterns & behavior
- User pain points and unmet needs

**Deliverables:**
- `reddit-philosophy-user-profiles.md` - Detailed user archetypes
- `user-behavior-patterns.md` - When, how, why they engage
- `crossover-communities-analysis.md` - What else they're interested in
- `pain-points-and-needs.md` - What problems they express
- `engagement-triggers.md` - What makes them participate vs lurk

### Agent 2: Demographics & Psychographics Deep Dive

## User Archetypes (Preliminary Framework)

Based on initial observations, we expect to find these archetypes in philosophy communities:

1. **The Student**: Asking homework questions, learning basics
2. **The Enthusiast**: Genuinely curious, reads philosophy for fun
3. **The Academic**: Has formal training, corrects misunderstandings
4. **The Debater**: Loves arguing, contrarian, challenges everything
5. **The Self-Helper**: Uses philosophy for life improvement (Stoicism)
6. **The Spiritual Seeker**: Philosophy as meaning-making
7. **The Intellectual**: Status-seeking, wants to appear smart

For each archetype, research will identify:
- What percentage of community?
- What motivates them?
- What frustrates them?
- What would make them join our app?
