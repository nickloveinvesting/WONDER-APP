## 1. BRAND IDENTITY

### What is ARGUED?

ARGUED is a competitive philosophical debate platform that treats argumentation as a learnable, measurable skill—similar to how Chess.com gamified chess. Users build reputation and earn DeLO ratings (philosophy's equivalent to Chess Elo) by making well-reasoned, evidence-backed philosophical arguments. Our platform democratizes intellectual discourse by removing popularity-based voting and replacing it with AI-judged evaluation of logical rigor, evidence quality, and rhetorical effectiveness.

### Brand Purpose

We exist to make philosophical thinking accessible, competitive, and rewarding. We reject the false binary of academic exclusivity versus social media chaos. ARGUED creates a middle ground: a structured intellectual space where argumentation quality is celebrated and measurable progress is real.

### Core Values

**Intellectual Rigor** - Arguments are judged on merit: logic, evidence, rhetoric. Not followers, likes, or mob consensus.

**Accessibility** - Philosophy belongs to everyone. We eliminate gatekeeping. A high school student and a professor compete on equal footing if their arguments are equally sound.

**Competitive Excellence** - Excellence is competitive. We celebrate winning—not as dominance, but as the mastery of persuasion and reasoning.

**Respectful Discourse** - Opposition strengthens arguments. We foster civil debate where disagreement is valued, not weaponized.

**Transparency** - AI judges explain their reasoning. Users understand why they won or lost. No algorithmic black boxes.

## 2. COLOR PALETTE & PSYCHOLOGY

### Primary Palette

| Color | Hex Code | Role | Psychology |
|-------|----------|------|-------------|
| **Navy Blue** | #1A3A52 | Primary interface, trust anchor | Trust, intellect, formality, stability |
| **Warm Brown** | #8B6F47 | Secondary accent, earned status | Sophistication, warmth, established authority |
| **Off-White (Cream)** | #F5F3F0 | Primary background | Elegance, accessibility, sophistication without coldness |
| **Rich Black** | #1C1C1C | Text, contrast, emphasis | Authority, clarity, contrast for readability |

### Why These Colors?

**Navy Blue (#1A3A52)**  
Navy signals trust, intellectual authority, and formal credibility. It's the color of law firms, academic institutions, and Chess.com's interface. For ARGUED, it represents the trustworthiness of our AI judgment system and the intellectual seriousness of our platform. Users should feel they're in a legitimate intellectual space, not a casual social feed.

**Warm Brown (#8B6F47)**  
Brown conveys sophistication, warmth, and earned status. Unlike gold (which feels cheap), brown suggests established heritage and academic tradition. We use it for achievements, badges, and earned reputation because brown feels *deserved*—it suggests something that's been earned through diligent work. This color also humanizes the platform; combined with cream, it avoids the sterile corporate feel of pure blue-and-white designs.

**Off-White Cream (#F5F3F0)**  
Pure white (#FFFFFF) feels cold, medical, corporate. Off-white cream creates warmth and sophistication while maintaining clarity. Think leather-bound philosophy books, aged paper in libraries, or the background of a well-designed academic journal. This background makes navy text highly readable while maintaining elegance. Users spend 20+ minutes per debate session—the warm background reduces eye strain and conveys thoughtfulness.

**Rich Black (#1C1C1C)**  
Not pure black (#000000), but rich black provides maximum contrast for readability while feeling slightly warmer than pure black. This is used for primary text, critical CTAs, and the logo. Black commands authority without feeling aggressive.

## 3. TONE & VOICE

### Brand Voice Characteristics

**Intellectual but Accessible**  
We speak like an educated mentor, not a professor gatekeeping knowledge. Complex ideas are explained clearly. Jargon is minimized unless necessary—and when necessary, it's defined.

*Example*: "Your argument's logic score (85/100) was strong, but you needed more empirical evidence. Try citing peer-reviewed sources in your next debate."

**Confident but Not Arrogant**  
We present the platform's capabilities clearly without overselling. We acknowledge that argumentation is hard and improve-able.

*Example*: "You lost this debate. Here's why your opponent's logic chain was stronger, and how you can improve your rhetorical strategy."

**Respectful of Opposition**  
We celebrate good arguments even from losing positions. Disagreement is valued.

*Example*: "Opposing arguments rated your evidence as weak (40/100), but your logical structure was sound (78/100). Strengthen your citations."

**Competitive, Not Hostile**  
We celebrate winning and losing with equal dignity. Competition is about mastery, not dominance.

*Example*: "You won this debate. Your rhetorical framing was 12 points ahead of your opponent. Here's what you did well—and where stronger evidence could have added more margin."

**Clear and Precise**  
Every word counts. We avoid marketing fluff, buzzwords, and emotional manipulation.

*Avoid*: "Join a vibrant community of thought leaders changing the discourse!"  
*Use*: "Compete with philosophers worldwide. Improve your argumentation skills. Build your DeLO rating."

### Tone Across Contexts

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Onboarding** | Encouraging, clear | "Welcome to ARGUED. Think of this like chess for philosophy—your DeLO rating will climb as your argumentation improves." |
| **Judgment Results** | Neutral, educational | "You won on Logic (88) and Evidence (76), but lost on Rhetoric (64). Here's why your opponent's framing was more persuasive." |
| **Error Messages** | Helpful, honest | "Your argument needs at least 150 characters. Add more detail to substantiate your position." |
| **Leaderboard** | Competitive, respectful | "Top 100 debaters this week. Your ranking: #247. You're 450 rating points from top 100." |
| **Community Features** | Collaborative, intellectual | "Join the Stoicism school. Discuss philosophy with 2,300+ debaters exploring ancient philosophy." |

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY

### Font Hierarchy

**Headlines & Logo Integration**  
Font: **Inter** (Sans-serif, geometric, modern)  
Weight: Bold (700) for main headlines  
Usage: Page titles, debate topic headers, brand logo contexts  
Psychology: Inter is modern, readable, and used by platforms like Figma and Linear. It signals contemporary intellectual thinking while remaining highly legible.

**Body Text & Arguments**  
Font: **Lora** (Serif, traditional, readable)  
Weight: Regular (400)  
Usage: Debate arguments, written content, explanations  
Psychology: Serif fonts (like academic journals) increase perceived credibility. Lora specifically feels warm and approachable compared to stiff academic serifs. Arguments are written *in* Lora to feel scholarly.

**UI Elements & Navigation**  
Font: **Inter** (Sans-serif)  
Weight: Medium (500)  
Usage: Buttons, labels, navigation, metadata  
Psychology: Sans-serif in UI maintains clarity and modernity while Inter specifically feels less corporate than Helvetica.

**Monospace for Data**  
Font: **Monaco** or **Courier New**  
Usage: Rating scores, stats, code snippets  
Psychology: Monospace conveys precision and data integrity.

### Size Scale

- **H1** (Page Title): 48px / Bold
- **H2** (Section Header): 36px / Bold
- **H3** (Subsection): 24px / Bold
- **Body Text**: 16px / Regular
- **Small Text** (metadata, timestamps): 12px / Regular
- **Button Text**: 16px / Medium

## 5. LOGO & BRAND MARK USAGE

### Logo Specifications

**Primary Logo**: Black speech bubble with white "ARGUED" text  
**Alternate Logo**: White "ARGUED" on transparent/colored background  

### Usage Guidelines

**DO:**
- Use black logo on cream/white backgrounds
- Use white logo on navy/dark backgrounds
- Maintain clear space around logo (minimum 10px)
- Scale proportionally (never distort)
- Use full logo on landing page hero, main navigation
- Use logo mark (just speech bubble) in favicon, app icons

**DON'T:**
- Change colors (use only black, white, or grayscale)
- Rotate or skew the logo
- Add drop shadows or effects
- Place on busy backgrounds without adequate contrast
- Use outdated versions

### Logo Placement Examples

| Location | Version | Background |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Header/Navigation | Black full logo | Cream/white |
| Dark footer | White full logo | Navy #1A3A52 |
| Mobile app icon | Mark only | Navy or cream |
| Landing page hero | Black full logo | Cream with brown accent |
| Email footer | Black logo 60px | White |

## 6. BRAND PERSONALITY & POSITIONING

### Who We're NOT

- Not academic elitism (gatekeeping philosophy)
- Not casual social media (no trending topics)
- Not entertainment (not engagement-farming)
- Not harsh competition (not toxic)
- Not obscure jargon (not gatekeeping through language)

### Who We ARE

We are the Chess.com of philosophy. We take argumentation seriously. We celebrate intellectual growth. We respect opposing viewpoints because they strengthen our arguments. We're for ambitious thinkers who want to compete, measure progress, and improve their reasoning skills.

Our user base includes philosophy students, curious professionals, academic enthusiasts, and competitive intellectuals. They're not here to doomscroll—they're here to think, compete, and grow.

## 7. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### Visual Hierarchy

1. Navy blue for primary actions and trust elements
2. Brown for earned achievements and secondary actions
3. Black for critical text and high-contrast elements
4. Cream background for clarity and warmth
5. Gray for disabled/secondary states

### Spacing & Breathing Room

The design should feel *spacious*, not cramped. Philosophical thinking requires clarity. Each debate argument should have clear visual separation. 

- Large padding around content (24px minimum)
- Generous whitespace between sections
- Cream background reduces need for borders (less "boxy")

### Interactivity

- Buttons: Navy background, white text, subtle hover effect (brown accent on hover)
- Links: Navy text, underline on hover
- Form inputs: Cream background with navy border
- Cards/sections: Subtle shadow on navy border (not heavy shadows)

## 9. DIFFERENTIATORS (Why This Branding?)

### Why Navy + Brown + Cream?

These three colors together signal: *Serious, warm, accessible, elite but not gatekeeping.*

- Navy alone = corporate, cold
- Brown alone = casual, earthy
- Cream alone = minimalist, sterile
- **Together** = intellectual heritage + modern warmth + accessibility

Compare to competitors:
- Chess.com: Green + white (friendly competition)
- Reddit: Alien orange (casual community)
- Academia.edu: Institutional gray (gatekeeping)

**ARGUED**: Navy + Brown + Cream = "prestigious but welcoming intellectual space"

### Why This Tone?

Our tone avoids the false dichotomy:
- Not "friendly startup" (Chess.com is friendly, but ARGUED is more serious)
- Not "cold academic" (journals are distant, but ARGUED is competitive)

We're competitive yet respectful because that's what real intellectual growth requires. You learn from opponents. You improve through rigorous feedback. You earn your rating through work, not entertainment.
