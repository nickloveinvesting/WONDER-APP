## PART 1: CURRENT ENGAGEMENT ANALYSIS

### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY - PART 1

**Current State**: ARGUED is a well-built philosophical debate platform with excellent AI judgment capabilities and professional design. However, it suffers from catastrophic time-to-value (24-168 hours vs. industry standard <5 minutes) and lacks essential retention mechanics.

**Core Issues**:
- Only ~1.5% of signups estimated to complete first debate
- Zero habit-forming triggers (no notifications, streaks, or daily mechanics)

## 5. FRICTION POINTS (Where Users Drop Off)

### Drop-off Point #1: Landing Page (~40% bounce rate estimated)
**Problem**: 552 lines of marketing copy before value
**Friction**: Information overload, unclear value prop buried in text
**User thinking**: "This looks interesting but I don't have time to read all this"

### Drop-off Point #2: After Signup (~50% drop estimated)
**Problem**: Dumped into /debates page with zero guidance
**Friction**: "What do I do now? Create? Browse? What topic?"
**User thinking**: "This is more work than I expected"

### Drop-off Point #3: Choosing First Action (~30% drop estimated)
**Problem**: Both options (create/join) require high commitment
**Friction**:
- Create = craft compelling topic, wait for others
- Join = pick side on someone else's topic (instant commitment)

**User thinking**: "I need to think about this... I'll come back later" (they won't)

### Drop-off Point #4: Writing First Argument (~40% drop estimated)
**Problem**: Blank text box, no guidance, high expectations
**Friction**: "How long? How formal? How do I structure this?"
**User thinking**: "I don't have time for this right now" (closes tab)

**Problem**: Days pass with zero engagement
**Friction**: No notifications, no alternative activities, no reason to check back
**User thinking**: *forgets app exists*

### Drop-off Point #6: Losing First Debate (~70% drop estimated)
**Problem**: Early loss craters motivation, especially for perfectionists
**Friction**: Win rate drops to 0%, feels bad, no recovery path shown
