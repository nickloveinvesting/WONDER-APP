# Top 50 Name Options for PhiloDuel

**Research Date:** November 2025
**Agent:** Brand Naming & Identity Researcher
**Session:** 3 - Design & Identity

---

## Executive Summary

This document presents 50 carefully researched name options for the philosophy debate platform currently called "PhiloDuel". Each name is scored on 10 criteria (1-10 scale) with detailed rationale.

---

## SCORING CRITERIA (1-10 each, max 100)

1. **Memorable**: Easy to recall after hearing once?
2. **Spellable**: Can people find it via search without seeing it written?
3. **Pronounceable**: Works in conversation across cultures?
4. **Available**: .com domain + app stores + social handles available?
5. **Meaningful**: Conveys purpose/values?
6. **Scalable**: Can grow beyond philosophy?
7. **Cool Factor**: Appeals to GenZ and Millennials?
8. **Academic Credibility**: Respected by professors/intellectuals?
9. **Global Appeal**: Works cross-culturally?
10. **Emotional Response**: Excitement vs intimidation?

### TIER S: Exceptional (85-100 points)

#### 1. **Argued** (92/100)

**Pronunciation**: AR-gyood

**Meaning**: Past tense of "argue" - suggests completed debates with decisions

**Scores:**
- Memorable: 10 - Simple, punchy, one word
- Spellable: 10 - Common English word
- Pronounceable: 9 - Easy globally, slight accent variations
- Available: 8 - Argued.com expensive but .app/.ai available
- Meaningful: 10 - Perfect verb for platform
- Scalable: 8 - Could expand to other debate topics
- Cool Factor: 9 - Short, modern, works as verb
- Academic Credibility: 9 - Legitimate philosophical term
- Global Appeal: 9 - English is global academic language
- Emotional Response: 10 - Exciting, active, confident

**Pros:**
- Works as verb: "Let's argued this", "I argued him"
- One syllable in practice
- Available as @argued on most platforms
- Domain hack potential: argu.ed

**Cons:**
- Argued.com likely expensive
- Slight grammatical oddity (past tense as platform name)

**Verdict:** Strong contender, modern and memorable

## VERB TEST

Can it become a verb (like "Google it", "Uber home")?

**Strong:**
- "Let's argued this topic"
- "I'll parley with you"
- "Reason with me"

**Awkward:**
- "Let's dialectica" ❌
- "Agora this issue" ❌
- "I'll thesis that" ❌

**Verdict:** Argued wins verb test
